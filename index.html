Council for Opportunity in Education

I started at the Council for Opportunity in Education--a national nonprofit that supports federal TRIO programs and advocates for first-generation and low-income students-—as a two-month intern, but was soon brought on full-time as a Project Assistant. During my year at the Council, I worked across government affairs affairs, research, and program and professional development, culminating in my role supporting the Council’s targeted state initiative, where I identified and pursued legislative advocacy opportunities for FGLI students across eight states.

Drafted constituent correspondence and outreach materials for district stakeholders and national advocacy partners. Engaged with federal agencies and congressional staff to track legislative priorities and strengthen constituent access to federal programs.
